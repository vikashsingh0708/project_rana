Lotus Plant

Scientific Name: Nelumbo nucifera

Family: Nelumbonaceae

Type: Aquatic Plant

Description: Lotus is an aquatic plant that grows in ponds and lakes. It is known for its ability to grow in muddy water while remaining clean.

Uses: Lotus is used in religious ceremonies, traditional medicine, and as food in some cultures. Its seeds and roots are edible.

Environmental Importance: Lotus helps maintain aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms and improving water quality.

Additional Facts: Lotus is the national flower of India and represents purity, strength, and resilience.
